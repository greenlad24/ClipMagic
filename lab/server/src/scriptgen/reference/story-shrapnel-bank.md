# Story Shrapnel Bank — Bar-Jake throwaway lines

**Last updated:** May 13, 2026

## How to use this

These are **throwaway lines** for video scripts, descriptions, and posts. Each one is a fragment of Jake's backstory delivered sideways — never as a monologue, never as a credential drop.

**Rules:**
1. **Max one shrapnel line per video.** Two is a monologue. One is texture.
2. **Drop sideways.** Mid-sentence, mid-thought. Never as an intro. Never announced.
3. **Always followed by a pivot.** "Anyway —" / "But —" / "That said —" / just keep going.
4. **Match the mood to the moment.** A self-deprecating line in a serious tutorial moment kills the flow. A dry line during a hype moment works.
5. **If it doesn't fit, don't force it.** Some videos have zero shrapnel. That's fine.
6. **Never use the same line twice in 30 days.** Track usage.

The whole point: viewers feel like they *know* Jake after 30 videos, without him ever sitting them down to tell them his story.

## 🪞 Self-deprecating (10 lines)

For moments when Jake is showing off a result or a smart move — the shrapnel keeps him human.

1. "I tried building this kind of thing years ago. Took me six months and the wrong year. Anyway —"
2. "I once paid 50 grand for a feature I could now build in an afternoon. Universe is healing."
3. "I've been wrong about more tech predictions than I'd like to admit publicly. The ones I got right were usually boring."
4. "My first startup hit 100 users. I called that a community. It was a chat room."
5. "I used to think I was good at picking timing. I was not."
6. "Built my first SaaS the hard way. Hired developers, spent real money, did it the way the books said. AI would've saved me a year and most of my dignity."
7. "I had a video Twitter idea six years before TikTok existed. Right idea, completely wrong decade."
8. "I've been using this tool for two weeks and I've already deleted half of what I built. Worth it."
9. "Spent more time naming this thing than building it. Don't judge me."
10. "I rebuilt this three times. The third version is the one I'm not embarrassed by."

## 👀 Observational (7 lines)

For moments that need a small reframe or a sideways comment — Jake noticing something the viewer might've missed.

11. "It's interesting how the things that look exciting in a demo are usually not the things you end up using daily."
12. "Every AI tool launches sounding like it'll change everything. Maybe one in ten actually does."
13. "The gap between what these tools promise and what they deliver out of the box is wider than anyone admits."
14. "Most of the wins from AI right now aren't from the flashy stuff. They're from the boring repeated tasks nobody talks about."
15. "Half the value of any new tool is figuring out which 10% of features you'll actually keep using."
16. "Demos are great. Real workflows are messier. Both can be true."
17. "Every new tool feels like a Saturday afternoon project until you try to make it serious."

---

## 🤔 Curious (4 lines)

For moments when Jake is mid-discovery — these earn the viewer's curiosity along with him.

18. "I went down a rabbit hole on this for a week and came back with a couple of things I didn't expect."
19. "Honestly, the thing that surprised me wasn't the feature. It was how I ended up using it."
20. "I've been quietly testing this for a few weeks. I wasn't sure if I'd recommend it. Now I am."
21. "Three weeks in, I noticed something I didn't expect — and once you see it, you can't unsee it."

---

## ☕ Dry (4 lines)

For moments that need a small comedic beat without breaking the flow.

22. "I worked as a UX engineer for a while, which mostly means I have very strong opinions about button placement that nobody asked for."
23. "I used to run a remote team across six time zones. I will never make jokes about meeting culture again. Meetings save lives."
24. "I've been writing about tech for ten years. The hype cycles are starting to rhyme."
25. "I spent three years writing about marketing. Most of that work is now obsolete. The boring parts aren't."

## 🎯 Pairing guide — which mood for which moment

| Video moment | Best shrapnel mood | Example combo |
|---|---|---|
| After showing an impressive AI result | Self-deprecating | "Cost me 50 grand to build the slow version of this in 2022." |
| During tool intro / setup | Observational | "Most of the wins right now aren't the flashy stuff." |
| Inside an experiment ("I tried…") | Curious | "I went down a rabbit hole for a week…" |
| In a slow stretch / mid-tutorial | Dry | "Strong opinions about button placement that nobody asked for." |
| Right before a sponsor segment | Observational or Dry | Keeps the tone calm — never self-deprecating right before recommending a tool |
| Hook / cold open | Curious | "I noticed something I didn't expect…" |
| Outro / wrap-up | Self-deprecating | Closes warm, leaves viewer wanting more |

## 🔁 Expansion rules

When adding new shrapnel:
- Must fit one of the four moods (self-deprecating / observational / curious / dry)
- Must be sayable in one breath
- Must follow the **"and then pivot away"** pattern — line, then move on
- Must pass the bar test: *would Jake say this to a stranger he just met and liked?*
- No credentials. No company names. No punch-sideways. No punch-down.
- If unsure, add it to a *draft* section first and use it in 2 videos before locking in
