HARD RULES — APPLY TO ALL FOUR FORMULAS
------------------------------------------------------------

Voice rules (non-negotiable):
1. Never punch sideways — no "most tutorials don't…", no competitor names, no "unlike other tools"
2. Never punch down — no "if you've been doing this wrong", no "most people don't know" framed as judgment (note: "most people don't know exist" is fine when framed as opportunity)
3. Self at the punchline, never the viewer or peers
4. No credential dump in Formula A-Long — credentials leak in sideways. Formula A-Compressed has a short narrative credential in Beat 2 (keep it under 20 words). Formula C has its own credential beat (Beat 4) which is allowed — keep it short.
5. No company name drops (Talkme, Weshare, etc.)
6. Welcome line goes AFTER the hook earned attention — never the opening line
7. Use "Turns out..." in Formula A-Long Beat 2 (signature reframe word)
8. Use "And here's the crazy part:" or "But here's the wild part:" to bridge Formula A-Long Beats 2→3
9. For Formula A-Compressed: lead with the visual outcome + "Look at this..." or equivalent. The first sentence states what's on screen.
10. For Formula B/C: lead with the metaphor + number. Always.

Specificity rules:
1. Every number from the outline must appear exactly (if outline says "25 things," use 25)
2. Tool names appear no earlier than Beat 3 in Formula A; in Formula B/C, the number is the headline (tool name is fine in Beat 3)
3. Pain / situation must be a SPECIFIC scenario, not generic
4. Deliverables must be CONCRETE things — a thumbnail, a workflow, a contract, a chart

Sentence rules:
1. One idea per sentence
2. Opening sentence: 5–12 words (Formula A starts with confession, Formula B/C start with metaphor question)
3. Contractions ("I'm" not "I am")
4. No tongue twisters — read aloud, fix anything that stumbles
5. 14-year-old reading level

Conversational texture rules (MKBHD-style, positive sparkle):
The hook should sound spoken, not presented. Use 2-3 (max) of these conversational moves IN THE HOOK to make it feel human — never stack, never sound performed. Match the formula:
1. Real-time thinking openers — "So…" / "Like…" / "I mean…" / "You know…" (use sparingly, 1-2 max in hook)
2. Soft hedge ownership — "…I think." / "…honestly." / "…in a good way." — one of these in the hook humanizes the dramatic claim
3. Credit-giving when the topic deserves it — "It's a genuinely smart idea." / "That's the part I keep coming back to." (only in Formula A hooks, not B/C)
4. Self-aware aside — "…to make sure I'm not crazy here…" / "…which is hilarious, in a good way." (max once, optional)
5. Curious framing instead of skeptical — "I'm curious-watching to see how this plays out." / "If they get this right, that's a real win." — replaces any negative framing

Examples of how this lands in a hook (compare two versions):

❌ Without conversational texture (sounds like a presenter):
"In this video, I'm going to walk you through 25 ChatGPT moves most people don't even know exist. By the end of this video, you'll be using ChatGPT on a completely different level."

✅ With conversational texture (sounds like Jake at the bar):
"So in this video, I'm walking you through 25 ChatGPT moves most people don't even know exist — with real examples you can copy today. And honestly, by the end of this video, you'll be using ChatGPT on a completely different level."

The difference: "So" (slot 3 opener), "honestly" (slot 4 soft hedge), and the em-dash aside ("with real examples you can copy today") — three tiny moves that make it sound like talking, not reading. Total time cost: zero seconds. Total credibility gain: significant.

Hard limits for hooks:
- Don't overdo it. The hook is short — 2-3 conversational moves max. More than that and the drama dies.
- Never use slot 4 hedge BEFORE the dramatic claim — it undercuts the punch. Always after.
- For Formula B/C (numbered listicles), the metaphor opening is sacred — don't soften it with "So…" or "Like…". Keep the metaphor sharp.
- For Formula A (confession reframe), conversational texture is welcome from sentence 1.

Audience framing:
1. Inclusive language — "anyone who's tried this", "people building businesses", "creators and operators"
2. Formula B/C: audience qualifier is EXPLICIT — name 2-4 viewer types by noun
3. NEVER limit to one demographic in a way that excludes — even when naming types, use them as examples not gates

Sponsored video rules — TWO DIFFERENT CASES:

CASE 1 — WHOLE-VIDEO SPONSORSHIP (entire video IS the sponsored content):
- NO verbal sponsor disclosure anywhere in the script
- Open the video organically, run it organically — viewers know the topic IS the sponsor (because the whole video is about the sponsor's product)
- Disclosure is handled by:
  (a) YouTube Studio "Paid promotion" checkbox at upload
  (b) "(sponsor)" label on the link in description
  (c) #ad / #sponsored hashtag in description if the sponsor requires it
- DO NOT add "Real quick, this video is sponsored by..." — it kills retention for no legal benefit. The Paid Promotion checkbox handles disclosure.
- Examples: Topview tool review, full Manus AI walkthrough, Softr tutorial, any single-tool deep-dive paid for by that tool's company

CASE 2 — SPONSOR SEGMENT inside an otherwise organic video (a 60-90 second sponsor read mid-video):
- Verbal disclosure IS required at the start of the segment
- Place the segment mid-roll, after first 5-7 minutes when retention is established (see Stage 4)
- Structure of the segment: situation → tool → demo → CTA
- Open the segment with a clean transition + ONE transparency sentence
- DO NOT put the sponsor segment inside the cold open beats — it kills retention
- Example: "Quick break — this segment is sponsored by NordVPN. Here's why it actually matters for what we just talked about..."

UNIVERSAL RULES (apply to both cases):
- NEVER use "stick around for the discount code" — promo codes go at end of video + description
- NEVER use "This video is sponsored by [X], and honestly..." — double-signals "ad" and kills retention
- For whole-video sponsorships, the title and thumbnail should be outcome-led ("I built X with Y") not "Y Review" — Review framing tanks retention
