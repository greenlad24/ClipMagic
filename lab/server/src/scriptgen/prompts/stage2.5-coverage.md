## STEP 1 — LIST WHAT THE BRIEF ASKED FOR

Go through the brief and pull out every distinct thing it asks the video to include. Look especially at any section titled "What TO Include", "The Product", "Key points", or similar — but a request counts wherever it appears, including one made in passing.

A request is something the video must **say or show**. It is not tone guidance, and it is not a "what NOT to include" ban.

Be specific. "The onboarding flow" is not a request — "the onboarding wow moment: enter your name + website, watch it build your profile, find your ICP, and train on your content in seconds" is.

## STEP 2 — JUDGE THE OUTLINE, ITEM BY ITEM

For each request, one of:

- **covered** — the outline already gives it a real home, in the depth the brief implied. Name the section.
- **added** — the outline drops it, glosses it in a passing clause, or buries it somewhere it will not survive drafting. You are going to fix this in Step 3.
- **gap** — you are deliberately NOT carrying it, and you can say exactly why.

Only three things justify a **gap**:

1. **The brief itself flags it as unconfirmed** ("get this in writing first", "confirm before naming", "only if they confirm it's live"). Honor that — record the gap, and do not invent the detail.
2. **It cannot be delivered without breaking a hard voice rule.** Be careful here, and read the rule before you claim it. A price the viewer PAYS is not an income claim — income claims are about money the VIEWER EARNS, and "what something costs" is explicitly allowed. A brief that says "$40/month is fine to use" is asking for a price, not a promise.
3. **It depends on footage or a live account that only Jake can supply.** Then it is still a section — write the section and mark the unconfirmed parts. It is not a reason to drop the topic.

"It didn't fit" is not a gap. "The outline felt long enough" is not a gap. If a request is real and the outline hasn't got it, it becomes a section.

## STEP 3 — REWRITE THE OUTLINE

Return the COMPLETE outline with every **added** item now carried properly.

- Give a dropped request **its own section**, at the depth the brief implied, in the place in the running order where it actually belongs. A wow-moment that opens the product story goes near the front, not bolted on at the end.
